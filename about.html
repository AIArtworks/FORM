Est. 2024 · The Engineering Archive

Form follows function.

FORM began with a simple conviction: the machines and structures that moved the world forward deserve to be drawn, catalogued, and hung — not photographed and forgotten.

What we make

An archive,
not a poster shop.

Each FORM piece is a considered engineering study — a machine or structure rendered by hand with a draughtsman's discipline, then catalogued with a study number, year, and accession code. It's the language of a museum collection, applied to the icons of design and engineering.

We start with the machines that changed everything — motorcycles first, then sports cars — and expand into mechanical anatomy, measured blueprints, the workshop, and industrial icons. One archive, expanding one volume at a time.
